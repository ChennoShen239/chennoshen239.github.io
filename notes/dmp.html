Unemployment Dynamics

Let the period length be \(\Delta\) and unemp. rate at time \(t\) be \(u\left(t\right)\): \[u\left(t+\Delta\right)=\sigma\Delta\left(1-u\left(t\right)\right)+\left(1-\lambda_{w}\Delta\right)u\left(t\right)\] where \(\sigma\Delta>0\) is the prob. of losing the job, \(\lambda_{w}\Delta>0\) be the prob. of finding a job over \(\Delta\).

We can rewrite it as: \[\begin{align*} u\left(t+\Delta\right) & =\sigma\Delta-\sigma\Delta u\left(t\right)+u\left(t\right)-\lambda_{w}\Delta u\left(t\right)\\ u\left(t+\Delta\right)-u\left(t\right) & =\sigma\Delta-\sigma\Delta u\left(t\right)-\lambda_{w}\Delta u\left(t\right)\\ \frac{u\left(t+\Delta\right)-u\left(t\right)}{\Delta} & =\sigma-\sigma u\left(t\right)-\lambda_{w}u\left(t\right)\\ \frac{u\left(t+\Delta\right)-u\left(t\right)}{\Delta} & =\sigma\left(1-u\left(t\right)\right)-\lambda_{w}u\left(t\right) \end{align*}\] so taking \(\Delta\to0\): \[u'\left(t\right)=\sigma\left(1-u\left(t\right)\right)-\lambda_{w}u\left(t\right)\]

We then try to endogenize job-finding rate \(\lambda_{w}\). Let agg. vacancy be \(v\) and the matching function takes the form of \[M\left(u,v\right)\Delta\] Some assumptions:

  1. matching is random

    1. all vacancies have an equal chance to match w/ an employed worker

    2. all unemployed workers have an equal chance to match w/ a vacancy

  2. \(M\) is increasing in both terms

  3. \(M\) is CRS

Under these assumptions, the prob. of a vacancy matching w/ an unemp. worker during \(\Delta\) is \[\frac{M\left(u,v\right)\Delta}{v}=M\left(\frac{1}{\theta},1\right)\Delta=\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)\Delta\] where \(\theta\equiv\frac{v}{u}\) is the tightness. The prob. of an unemp. worker matching w/ a vacancy during \(\Delta\) is: \[\frac{M\left(u,v\right)\Delta}{u}=M\left(1,\theta\right)\Delta=\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)\Delta\] and \(\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)=M\left(1,\theta\right)=\theta M\left(\frac{1}{\theta},1\right)=\theta\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right).\) So unemp. rate dynamics now become: \[u'\left(t\right)=\sigma\left(1-u\left(t\right)\right)-\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)u\left(t\right)\]

Beveridge curve condition

Consider ss. whre \(v\left(t\right)\equiv v,\forall t\) and \(u'\left(t\right)=0\): \[\begin{align*} 0 & =\sigma\left(1-u\right)-\lambda_{w}\left(\frac{v}{u}\right)u\\ \lambda_{w}\left(\frac{v}{u}\right)u & =\sigma\left(1-u\right)\\ u\left(\sigma+\lambda_{w}\left(\frac{v}{u}\right)\right) & =\sigma\\ u & =\frac{\sigma}{\sigma+\lambda_{w}\left(\frac{v}{u}\right)} \end{align*}\]

This \(u=\frac{\sigma}{\sigma+\lambda_{w}\left(\frac{v}{u}\right)}\) is referred to as the Beveridge curve condition.

DMP Model

Workers and Firms

The DMP builds on the unemp. dynamics and adds the endo. determination of agg. vacancy \(v\left(t\right)\). Firms create vacancies by trading off the current vacancy posting cost and future profit from the firm-worker match.

We assume that each vacancy hires one worker. Let \(w\left(t\right)\) be the eq. wage. The PV of being employed, in discrete-time setting w/ period length \(\Delta\) is \[\begin{equation} W\left(t\right)=w\left(t\right)\Delta+\frac{1}{1+r\Delta}\left(\left(1-\sigma\Delta\right)W\left(t+\Delta\right)+\sigma\Delta U\left(t+\Delta\right)\right)\label{eq:W} \end{equation}\] where \(U\left(t\right)\) is the value of being unemployed. \[U\left(t\right)=b\Delta+\frac{1}{1+r\Delta}\left(\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)\Delta W\left(t+\Delta\right)+\left(1-\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)\Delta\right)U\left(t+\Delta\right)\right)\]

Multiplying \(1+r\Delta\) on both sides of [eq:W] we get: \[\begin{align*} \left(1+r\Delta\right)W\left(t\right) & =\left(1+r\Delta\right)w\left(t\right)\Delta+\left(\left(1-\sigma\Delta\right)W\left(t+\Delta\right)+\sigma\Delta U\left(t+\Delta\right)\right)\\ r\Delta W\left(t\right) & =\left(1+r\Delta\right)w\left(t\right)\Delta+\left(\left(1-\sigma\Delta\right)W\left(t+\Delta\right)+\sigma\Delta U\left(t+\Delta\right)\right)-W\left(t\right)\\ rW\left(t\right) & =\left(1+r\Delta\right)w\left(t\right)+\sigma U\left(t+\Delta\right)+\left(\frac{1}{\Delta}-\sigma\right)W\left(t+\Delta\right)-\frac{1}{\Delta}W\left(t\right)\\ rW\left(t\right) & =\left(1+r\Delta\right)w\left(t\right)+\sigma\left(U\left(t+\Delta\right)-W\left(t+\Delta\right)\right)+\frac{W\left(t+\Delta\right)-W\left(t\right)}{\Delta} \end{align*}\]

so taking \(\Delta\to0\): \[rW\left(t\right)=w\left(t\right)+\sigma\left(U\left(t\right)-W\left(t\right)\right)+W'\left(t\right)\] Similarly for \(U\left(t\right)\): \[\begin{align*} \left(1+r\Delta\right)U\left(t\right) & =\left(1+r\Delta\right)b\Delta+\left(\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)\Delta W\left(t+\Delta\right)+\left(1-\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)\Delta\right)U\left(t+\Delta\right)\right)\\ r\Delta U\left(t\right) & =\left(1+r\Delta\right)b\Delta+\left(\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)\Delta W\left(t+\Delta\right)+\left(1-\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)\Delta\right)U\left(t+\Delta\right)\right)-U\left(t\right)\\ rU\left(t\right) & =\left(1+r\Delta\right)b+\left(\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)W\left(t+\Delta\right)-\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)U\left(t+\Delta\right)\right)+\frac{U\left(t+\Delta\right)-U\left(t\right)}{\Delta} \end{align*}\] so taking \(\Delta\to0\): \[rU\left(t\right)=b+\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)\left(W\left(t\right)-U\left(t\right)\right)+U'\left(t\right)\]

Because of the one-firm to one-worker assumption, the firm is either matched or vacant, the value of a matched firm is: \[J\left(t\right)=p\left(t\right)\Delta-w\left(t\right)\Delta+\frac{1}{1+r\Delta}\left(\sigma\Delta V\left(t+\Delta\right)+\left(1-\sigma\Delta\right)J\left(t+\Delta\right)\right)\] where \(p\left(t\right)\Delta\)is the product produced by the match and \(V\left(t\right)\) is the value of vacancy, computed by: \[V\left(t\right)=-\kappa\Delta+\frac{1}{1+r\Delta}\left(\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)\Delta J\left(t+\Delta\right)+\left(1-\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)\Delta\right)V\left(t+\Delta\right)\right)\] where \(\kappa\Delta\) is the cost of posting a vacancy. It’s then easy to see that \[rJ\left(t\right)=p\left(t\right)-w\left(t\right)+\sigma\left(V\left(t\right)-J\left(t\right)\right)+J'\left(t\right)\] and \[\begin{equation} rV\left(t\right)=-\kappa+\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)\left(J\left(t\right)-V\left(t\right)\right)+V'\left(t\right)\label{eq:V} \end{equation}\]

We assume that anyone can start a firm by posting a vacancy

  1. if \(V\left(t\right)>0\), new entrants would keep posting vacancies until \(V\left(t\right)\) becomes \(0\). Additional vacancies would drop the value of \(V\left(t\right)\)because \(\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)\) is a decreasing function of \(\theta\left(t\right)=\frac{v\left(t\right)}{u\left(t\right)}\). Therefore \(V\left(t\right)=0\) has to always hold, as long as \(v\left(t\right)>0\). 1

  2. We call the condition \[V\left(t\right)=0\] the free-entry condition

One can formalize 1 as follows:

Let \(u\left(t\right)\) be the state var. at time \(t\), which evolves as \[u'=\sigma\left(1-u\right)-\lambda_{w}u\]

and \(v\left(t\right)\)be the control var. and \(v\ge0\).Given [eq:V] and taking \(u\left(t\right),J\left(t\right),V'\left(t\right)\)as given, we have \[V\left(t\right)=\frac{\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)J\left(t\right)-\kappa+V'\left(t\right)}{r+\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)}\] so \[\frac{\partial V}{\partial\lambda_{f}}=\frac{J\left(t\right)\left(r+\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)\right)-\left(\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)J\left(t\right)-\kappa+V'\left(t\right)\right)}{\left(r+\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)\right)^{2}}\]

at ss., \(V'\left(t\right)=0\), so we have \[\begin{align*} \frac{\partial V}{\partial\lambda_{f}} & =\frac{rJ\left(t\right)+\kappa-V'\left(t\right)}{\left(r+\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)\right)^{2}}\\ & =\frac{rJ\left(t\right)+\kappa}{\left(r+\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\left(t\right)\right)\right)^{2}}>0 \end{align*}\]

Given the matching function, it’s easy to see that \(\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)=M\left(\frac{1}{\theta},1\right)\) is decreasing in \(\theta\): \[\frac{\partial\lambda_{f}}{\partial v}=\lambda_{f}'\left(\theta\right)\frac{1}{u}<0\]

so \[\frac{\partial V}{\partial v}=\frac{\partial V}{\partial\lambda_{f}}\frac{\partial\lambda_{f}}{\partial v}<0\]

Therefore, if \(V\left(t\right)>0\), then it follows that vacancy goes up and in turn pushing \(V\left(t\right)\to0\). If \(V\left(t\right)<0\), then nobody is willing to post a vacancy and in turn \(v\left(t\right)=0\). Finally if \(V\left(t\right)=0\), then entrants are indifferent and therefore \(\forall v\left(t\right)\ge0,s.t.V\left(v\left(t\right)\right)=0\) will work.

Wage Determination

In this framework, for a worker to meet w/ another firm, the worker has to endure the cost of unemployment. Similarly, for the firm, losing a worker would imply waiting for another worker by incurring the vacancy cost. Thus, the matched worker and firm are in a bilateral monopoly situation.

Here we assume that the surplus is split by the level of wage that solves the following maximization problem: \[\begin{equation} \max_{w}\left(\tilde{W}\left(w,t\right)-U\left(t\right)\right)^{\gamma}\left(\tilde{J}\left(w,t\right)-V\left(t\right)\right)^{1-\gamma}\label{eq:nash} \end{equation}\] where \(\gamma\in\left(0,1\right)\). The functions \(\tilde{W}\left(w,t\right)\) and \(\tilde{J}\left(w,t\right)\)are the values of employed workers and a matched firm, here we make it explicit that only \(w\) in this period can move. The solution to this problem is often called Generalized Nash Bargaining Rule.

The functions \(\tilde{W}\left(w,t\right)\) and \(\tilde{J}\left(w,t\right)\) can be written as: \[\tilde{W}\left(w,t\right)=w\Delta+\frac{1}{1+r\Delta}\left(\left(1-\sigma\Delta\right)W\left(t+\Delta\right)+\sigma\Delta U\left(t+\Delta\right)\right)\] and \[\tilde{J}\left(w,t\right)=p\left(t\right)\Delta-w\Delta+\frac{1}{1+r\Delta}\left(\sigma\Delta V\left(t+\Delta\right)+\left(1-\sigma\Delta\right)J\left(t+\Delta\right)\right).\]

By taking FOC for [eq:nash], we have: \[\begin{align*} \max_{w} & \gamma\ln\left(\tilde{W}\left(w,t\right)-U\left(t\right)\right)+\left(1-\gamma\right)\ln\left(\tilde{J}\left(w,t\right)-V\left(t\right)\right)\\ 0 & =\frac{\gamma}{\tilde{W}\left(w,t\right)-U\left(t\right)}\frac{\partial\tilde{W}\left(w,t\right)}{\partial w}+\frac{1-\gamma}{\tilde{J}\left(w,t\right)-V\left(t\right)}\frac{\partial\tilde{J}\left(w,t\right)}{\partial w} \end{align*}\]

Given \(\frac{\partial\tilde{W}\left(w,t\right)}{\partial w}=\Delta,\frac{\partial\tilde{J}\left(w,t\right)}{\partial w}=-\Delta\), we have \[\begin{align} 0 & =\frac{\gamma}{\tilde{W}\left(w,t\right)-U\left(t\right)}\Delta-\frac{1-\gamma}{\tilde{J}\left(w,t\right)-V\left(t\right)}\Delta\nonumber \\ 0 & =\frac{\gamma}{\tilde{W}\left(w,t\right)-U\left(t\right)}-\frac{1-\gamma}{\tilde{J}\left(w,t\right)-V\left(t\right)}\nonumber \\ \frac{1-\gamma}{\tilde{J}\left(w,t\right)-V\left(t\right)} & =\frac{\gamma}{\tilde{W}\left(w,t\right)-U\left(t\right)}\nonumber \\ \left(1-\gamma\right)\left(\tilde{W}\left(w,t\right)-U\left(t\right)\right) & =\gamma\left(\tilde{J}\left(w,t\right)-V\left(t\right)\right)\label{eq:foc} \end{align}\]

so the \(w^{*}\)that satisfies [eq:foc] is the eq. wage.

Steady State Equilibrium

First, consider the ss. eq. where \(u\left(t\right)\) and \(v\left(t\right)\) are constant. With the conditions above we have \[\begin{align*} rW & =w-\sigma\left(W-U\right)\\ rU & =b+\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)\left(W-U\right)\\ rJ & =p-w-\sigma\left(J-V\right)\\ rV & =-\kappa+\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)\left(J-V\right)\\ V & =0\\ \left(1-\gamma\right)\left(W-U\right) & =\gamma\left(J-V\right) \end{align*}\]

in total 6 equations w/ 6 unknowns \(W,U,J,V,\theta,w\). We can then try to reduce this system to 1 to 1. It can be shown that we have \[(1-\gamma)(p-b)-\frac{r+\sigma+\gamma\lambda_{w}(\bar{\theta})}{\lambda_{f}(\bar{\theta})}\kappa=0.\] This condition is then used to solve for \(\theta\) and is often referred to as the job creation condition.

For simple math, we have \[\begin{align*} rV & =-\kappa+\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)\left(J-V\right)\\ 0 & =-\kappa+\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)\left(J-0\right)\\ J & =\frac{\kappa}{\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)} \end{align*}\] Given \[\begin{align*} rJ & =p-w-\sigma\left(J-V\right)\\ rJ & =p-w-\sigma J\\ J & =\frac{p-w}{r+\sigma} \end{align*}\] we have the free-entry condition on firm side: \[\frac{\kappa}{\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)}=\frac{p-w}{r+\sigma}\]

Given \[\begin{align*} rW & =w-\sigma\left(W-U\right)\\ rU & =b+\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)\left(W-U\right)\\ r\left(W-U\right) & =w-b-\left(\sigma+\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)\right)\left(W-U\right)\\ \left(r+\sigma+\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)\right)\left(W-U\right) & =w-b\\ W-U & =\frac{w-b}{r+\sigma+\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)} \end{align*}\]

so given \(\left(1-\gamma\right)\left(W-U\right)=\gamma\left(J-V\right)\), we have \[\begin{align*} \left(1-\gamma\right)\frac{w-b}{r+\sigma+\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)} & =\gamma\frac{\kappa}{\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)}\\ w-b & =\frac{\gamma}{1-\gamma}\frac{\kappa}{\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)}\left(r+\sigma+\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)\right)\\ p-w & =p-\left[\frac{\gamma}{1-\gamma}\frac{\kappa}{\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)}\left(r+\sigma+\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)\right)+b\right]\\ \left(r+\sigma\right)\frac{\kappa}{\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)} & =p-\left[\frac{\gamma}{1-\gamma}\frac{\kappa}{\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)}\left(r+\sigma+\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)\right)+b\right]\\ \left(r+\sigma\right)\kappa & =\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)\left(p-b\right)-\frac{\gamma\kappa}{1-\gamma}\left(r+\sigma+\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)\right)\\ \left(r+\sigma\right)\kappa+\frac{\gamma\kappa}{1-\gamma}\left(r+\sigma+\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)\right) & =\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)\left(p-b\right)\\ \left(1-\gamma\right)\left(r+\sigma\right)\kappa+\gamma\kappa\left(r+\sigma+\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)\right) & =\left(1-\gamma\right)\left(p-b\right)\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)\\ \left(r+\sigma\right)\kappa+\gamma\kappa\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right) & =\left(1-\gamma\right)\left(p-b\right)\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)\\ \frac{r+\sigma+\gamma\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)}{\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)}\kappa & =\left(1-\gamma\right)\left(p-b\right) \end{align*}\] which is exactly the job creation condition. To explain this: \[\underbrace{\frac{r+\sigma+\gamma\lambda_{w}\left(\theta\right)}{\lambda_{f}\left(\theta\right)}}_{\text{annuitized job creation cost}}\kappa=\underbrace{\left(1-\gamma\right)\left(p-b\right)}_{\text{firm's share of match surplus}}\]

Given \(v=\theta u\), the dynamic of \(v'\left(t\right)\) is similar.
